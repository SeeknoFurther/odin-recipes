Description

This dish evokes the northwoods. Wild bird, wild mushrooms, wild cranberries. Something to aspire to in the fall.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups wild rice (divided- 1 cup for the dish, .5 cup for breading
  • 3 cups chicken stock
  • Breasts from 4 grouse
  • Salt
  • 4 tablespoons butter (divided- 3 tbs to saute the breasts, 1 tbs to saute the mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup rye, barley, or whole wheat flour
  • 1 or 2 pounds fresh mushrooms
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 cup cranberries
  • 1/4 cup fruit syrup
  • 1/3 cup cider vinegar
    •

    Preparation

    1. Salt the grouse breasts and set aside at room temperature
    2. Simmer 1 cup of the wild rice in the broth until tender, 20-50 minutes depending on the type of wild rice you have. When tender, drain and set aside in a covered bowl.
    3. Grind the remaining 1/2 cup of wild rice in a spice grinder into a powder. Mix with flourand dredge the grouse breasts in the it.
    4. Heat the 3 tablespoons of the butter in a large saute pan and saute the grouse breats until just barely done, 4-5 minutes per side. Remove the meat and set aside.
    5. Put the remaining tablespoon of butter in the pan and turn the heat to high. Add mushrooms, shaking the pan to prevent sticking. Cook until the mushrooms give up their water. Season with salt, add the garlic and thyme. Sear for 1-2 minutes. Stir the mushrooms until they are good and browned.
    6. Add the cranberries and toss to combine. Cook until the cranberries start popping, then add wild rice, vingar, and fruit syrup. Toss to combine and serve with the grouse.
